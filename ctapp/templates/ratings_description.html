Description of data quality ratings

In an effort to expose the varied quality of clinical trial information that is submitted to ClinicalTrials.gov, we created a system to rate each trial on the data quality measures listed below.

Institutional data quality ratings represent a simple average of the ratings for every trial associated with an institution. This includes all trials sponsored by and hosted at the institution and may not represent the quality of data submitted by individual departments or offices within that institution.

Please contact us with any further questions.

Negative Factors

Site data quality
How well are the trial's research site locations described?

  • Valid city and country

  • ZIP Code, if in the United States

  • A meaningful site name, e.g. "SF General Hospital"

  • A valid investigator name, e.g. "John Doe, MD"

  • A generic site name, e.g. "Investigation Site #24"

  • A recruiting status that is inconsistent with the trial's recruiting status

Protocol description quality
How much information is contained in the trial protocol description?

  • Approximately 500 words or more

  • Boilerplate text

  • A high number of very frequent terms, e.g. "trial", "study", "intervention"

Trial criteria description quality
How well are the trial's eligibility criteria described?

  • A section clearly labeled "inclusion criteria"

  • A section clearly labeled "exclusion criteria"

  • More than 30 eligibility criteria

Medical Subject Headings (MeSH) classification quality
How well are the trial's interventions and conditions described according to this controlled vocabulary?

  • Four or more condition terms

  • Two or more intervention terms

  • No condition terms

  • No intervention terms

Date quality
Do the trial's dates appear to be valid?

  • Presence of start and completion dates

  • Presence of completion date type, e.g. "Actual", "Anticipated"

  • Obviously incorrect dates, e.g. "December 2099"

  • "Anticipated" completion date occurs in the past
